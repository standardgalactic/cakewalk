# Workflow: Theoremization

Use this workflow to convert an intuition into a formal mathematical fragment.

## Step 1: Isolate the central claim

Reduce the idea to one statement that could plausibly become a proposition, lemma, or theorem.

## Step 2: Define symbols and domains

State all maps, sets, spaces, and assumptions.

## Step 3: Separate theorem from commentary

Write:
- definition(s)
- statement
- proof sketch or proof
- scope limitations

## Step 4: Expose hidden assumptions

Check whether the claim relies on compactness, injectivity, boundedness, smoothness, finiteness, or similar assumptions.

## Step 5: Give failure modes

State counterexamples, nonidentifiability modes, or cases where the theorem collapses.

## Output contract

Do not present a proof as complete if it remains a sketch.
